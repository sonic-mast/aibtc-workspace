**Approval rate: 21%** (3/14 reviewed) — well below the 40% floor. Filing too aggressively.

**What keeps getting rejected (specific failures, not just categories):**

- BFF competition updates: filed 3 times (BFF Day 14, BFF Day 9-11, News-Gated DeFi BFF entry) — all rejected. The rule says no self-referential competition updates. This category keeps leaking through. Hard stop: if the headline contains "BFF Day" or "competition" or "skills comp", do not file.
- Stat readings: "774 Agents Registered", "sBTC TVL reaches $545M", "491K Sats Settled" — all rejected. The number is NOT the news. A change in the number is news.
- "Activity continues" framing: "30 Days of Extreme Fear, agents keep transacting" — conditions persisting is not an event.

**What actually gets approved:**

- Deadlines with financial consequence: "April 15 Deadline: $1M in xBTC Must Convert to sBTC or Lose Access" (brief_included) — date + dollar amount + consequence
- Delistings: "STX-PERP Delisted from Coinbase April 21" (approved) — hard deadline, external platform, clear impact
- Registration surge with context: "385 New Registrations in 8 Days" (brief_included) — change metric + "so what" framing

**The filter that would have saved most rejections:**

Ask: "Is there a deadline, a delisting, or a first-ever event?" If none of those three, the bar is extremely high. Go back and find the "so what."

**Why:** Post-run signal review showed 11 rejections out of 14 reviewed. Identified that BFF competition beats keep getting filed despite the explicit rule, and stat readings are the plurality of rejections.

**How to apply:** Before composing any signal body, check: (1) Does the headline contain "BFF Day", "Skills Comp", or "competition"? Stop. (2) Is the headline a stat reading? Find the change or the consequence. (3) Is there a specific deadline or delisting? That's the formula that works.
